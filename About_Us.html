Who Are We?

Arjun Kumar

Hi I’m Arj. Originally hailing from India having moved to Australia in 2002 I spent most of my childhood in various locations in Australia ranging from Alice Springs to Hobart and finally in Launceston, Tasmania. I moved to Melbourne to pursue further studies and have called it home since. I follow a number of sports both as a hobby and an interest although I particularly enjoy soccer as I am a keen Liverpool supporter.

My interest in IT stems from several sources. Primarily I got into computers through gaming. As a result I started to learn how computers work in order to eek out as much performance as possible from my ancient rig. My dad was also quite interested in new technologies as they were made available and this further enabled me to explore new things such as smartphones, iPods/Pads, wireless tech, etc. without the associated costs to bear. Over the years technology has moved at a rapid pace and it is something I still know very little about. Thus I hope to expand my knowledge and get a better understanding of the IT sphere through my studies here at RMIT.

I possess very little in terms of job specific experience in the IT field. I have tinkered with hardware, played with BIOS settings and have a good understanding when it comes to computer hardware. However I have never really ventured into the software side of things apart from a brief stint with Photoshop and video editing.

Arjun Kumar

My Myers-Briggs test result indicated that I am an Assertive Advocate, INFJ. My Learning Styles Test indicated that I am primarily a Visual Learner. My Big Five Personality test score was in the high percentiles for Emotional Stability and Conscientiousness, median scores for Agreeableness and Intellect/Imagination (indicating more conventional rather than imaginative thinker) and quite a low score for Extroversion.

Utilising the information garnered from my test results. I am an organized and diligent worker who likes a workplace where there is a schedule and clear outline of the tasks that need to be performed. I am mindful of the people around me and would like to see them succeed as much as I would like to succeed myself. I believe this is a great quality in a team environment where the success of the group outweighs the success of the individual.

I am unlikely to let people face unnecessary hardships and will bring such matters to light however I am unlikely to create conflict unless I see a great wrong being done. I am empathetic and sensitive and thus an integral part of a group dynamic. But as I hold myself to such a high regard, I might also be quite sensitive to constructive criticism and might not always take it well. I will absorb information best through visual aids rather than audio. I will also get better at tasks the more I do them. I am likely to be a calm presence in a team dynamic. A voice of reason.

Simon Bird

As an ISFP, I enjoy life as it is and try to avoid conflict. I tend to be fairly outgoing, but very reserved, and I might take some time to open up around other people. I abhor narcissism, favouritism and superiority while favouring honesty and acceptance.

My result from a VARK learning style questionnaire is multimodal. I prefer to learn through visual, kinematic and reading/writing methods.

My result from the Kellogg North-western University’s Creativity Test is very creative with a score of 70 out of 116. I prefer to innovate – or think outside the box – when trying to solve problems. This is likely the leading factor of my infatuation for programming, doodling and game design.

My personality can evidently make or break a group. I prefer not to lead and hate being the centre of attention. I love to help others, but I feel shut down if my efforts are ignored or criticised. Therefore, I need a reciprocating team, lest I feel unwanted and avoid collaborating out of fear of rejection. If my efforts do go noticed, I can be a powerhouse where I strive to improve the quality of life of my colleagues.

This gives me the opportunity to oversee collaborative services and tools and assist other members with troubleshooting and general advice. For example, I could manage the Microsoft Teams group and GitHub repositories, aiding with such tools to those who need it.

Simultaneously, my creative drive enables me to contribute to design elements of documents, programs and sites with the team. For example, I could help design the project website and report. I can quickly find solutions to programming problems, assist with conventional mistakes and compile material from other members.
