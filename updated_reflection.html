Our Thoughts After the Prototype


Liam Folie

I am satisfied with the final outcomes. We worked well together. The team made an effort to help each other out when needed. There was constructive feedback when we shared our ideas. The experience has taught me that there is so much work involved in the process of mobile app development.

There could have been improvements in our communication and organization of files. We all have different methods of how we do things. If we were able to develop a consistent method across these areas we may have been able to work with increase efficiency.

Github is still moderately confusing for me to use in a group setting. I made multiple mistakes when trying to make commits that affect the main files. Fortunately, Simon was able to revert these changes. I just found the terminology confusing. With more practice, I am sure it will become easier to navigate.


Simon Bird

I believe that as a team, we work very well together. We are collaborative, yet we have initiative. We are modest, yet we understand our strengths and weaknesses. Despite one of our team members disappearing on us, we remained determined to produce a meaningful project outcome.

I think we could have improved our communication more than we hoped to, based on prior feedback. There were some hiccups regarding files and formats, app bugs, etc., that could have been amended faster using collaborative tools like OneNote (nobody likes the OneNote ☹).

The amount of work needed to create a simple mobile app was quite surprising, as was having one of our group members leave mid-project. Despite this, I believe we managed to pull through and create a decent project in the end.along the way.


Arjun Kumar

Coming into this project we had new and fresh ideas about how to organise ourselves to avoid the hurdles we encountered as a group last time. Through the initial phase of our project, we managed to incorporate some of these ideas into our meetings and workflow. However, due to work commitments outside of this project and a group member going AWOL things started to break down a little bit over time.

The communication aspect between the remaining members was still good but the deliverables were not always addressed in a timely fashion. This again led to a crunch time near the submission due date. This could also partly be blamed on the remaining members having to shoulder the workload that was left behind by Alan leaving. This time of year is quite busy for university work and this placed an extra burden on time management.

Our final approach was also not as systematic as it could have been in order to avoid having to chase people up for work at the end. It was also apparent when collating that many aspects were written from a person’s POV rather than as the group. This required a lot of editing in order to maintain a cohesive group report.

At the end of the day, we still managed to deliver the outcome that we set out to achieve and that is something we can be happy and proud of. Even through the problems we faced we still managed to meet the expectations that we placed on ourselves.


Original Reflection


Our Final Thoughts

Communication, collaboration, quality of work, and transparency between remaining group members were all aspects of the project that went well. Time management, writing approach and meeting the brief were all things that could have been done better. This could have led to a more seamless transition when collating and bringing everything together in the final document and website.

Surprisingly even though we lost a team member during this project we managed to rally and produce the outcome that we had initially set out to achieve. In essence, there was little lost.

All in all even though there is definite room for improvement we worked quite well as a team in challenging circumstances. The goals that we set were never altered and managing to achieve them leads us to believe that what we have done is something to be proud of.
