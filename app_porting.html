Mobile App Porting

Arjun Kumar

Mobile App Developer


I was in charge of packaging the app for mobile use. I researched the best approach to achieve this outcome and supplied Simon with the requirements in order to be able to achieve the desired outcome. I was also in charge of setting up the app packaging from Python into an APK that would function on an Android smartphone.


Scope and Limits

Considering app development, we decided to abandon the hope of developing an app in its native language as it required time and abilities far outside of our reach. None of us have experience with mobile app-specific programming languages and trying to learn one in 3 weeks was not feasible in my opinion. Our approach was to achieve a tangible outcome that we could physically present as a mobile app. This meant focusing on achieving a barebones prototype that would emulate our final desired app but in a very, very basic sense. This would give us a physical product to display rather than still working in drafts and ideas.

Given more time we could look at implementing more features into our prototype however it is quite likely our process would be dramatically different if we were working within a larger timeframe. The idea was to achieve a deliverable over implementing infrastructure for the future development of our product. In that sense, our goals would likely shift if we look at achieving our ideal goal and a lot of the work done here would not be what would be required for the final product.

Testing

The true test for our app will be using it in conjunction with the physical lockers. This will require a working prototype of both platforms to be able to accomplish. This will likely only be achievable near the end stages of development as it would require both hardware and software to be in a near finished state.

This will allow us to test the product in a real-world environment in order to receive user feedback regarding our product as well as our approach. The earlier we are able to achieve this the better however this is completely dependent on our approach and what aspects we aim to prioritize.
